## Why Digital Product Sellers Owe Quarterly Taxes

If you earn income from selling digital products — like courses, templates, eBooks, or software — and no taxes are withheld, you’re responsible for:

- ✅ Federal income tax  
- ✅ Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)  
- ✅ State income tax (if applicable)

And the IRS expects you to pay those taxes **quarterly**, not just once a year.

## What Counts as a Digital Product?

If you sell any of the following, you likely owe estimated taxes:

- Online courses (via Teachable, Kajabi, Podia)  
- Notion templates  
- Canva designs or social media kits  
- eBooks and PDF guides  
- Software, apps, or plug-ins  
- Audio or video downloads  
- Printables or worksheets  
- Stock photos or graphics  
- Website themes or landing pages

✅ Whether you're using Gumroad, Shopify, Lemon Squeezy, or your own site — if you're collecting revenue, it’s taxable.

## Who Needs to Pay Quarterly Taxes?

You’re required to make estimated tax payments if:

- You expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in federal tax for the year  
- You don’t have taxes withheld by a platform (most don’t)  
- You’re self-employed, even part-time

Even if digital products are your side hustle, the IRS treats it like a business.

## Step-by-Step: How to Calculate What You Owe

### Step 1: Estimate Your Annual Income

Add up your projected earnings across all platforms.

✅ Include revenue from:

- Product sales  
- Affiliate links or sponsorships  
- Upsells or add-ons

### Step 2: Subtract Business Expenses

Common write-offs for digital product sellers:

- Platform fees (Gumroad, Podia, etc.)  
- Software (Canva, Notion, Zapier)  
- Design tools or templates  
- Email marketing platforms  
- Domain, hosting, or website services  
- Contractor help or VAs  
- Home office portion

✅ Subtract these to get your **net profit**.

### Step 3: Apply a Tax Rate

Multiply your profit by **25–30%** to estimate your total tax obligation.

- ~15.3% self-employment tax  
- Plus federal income tax (10–24% for most sellers)

Example:

- $50,000 profit × 25% = $12,500 estimated tax

### Step 4: Divide by 4

Split your estimated taxes into four quarterly payments.

- $12,500 ÷ 4 = $3,125 per quarter

This is your base number — recalculate each quarter if income changes.

## When Are Quarterly Taxes Due?

Quarterly deadlines:

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15** (of the next year)

✅ Late payments can lead to penalties — even if you pay in full at tax time.

## What If Sales Are Inconsistent?

That’s normal. Just recalculate each quarter.

✅ Made more than expected? Pay a bit more  
✅ Had a slow month? Pay a bit less

Quarterly taxes are based on **what you expect to owe for the year** — and you can update that as you go.

## Where to Pay

- [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal taxes  
- Your state’s website for state estimated tax payments  
- Keep receipts and payment confirmations
