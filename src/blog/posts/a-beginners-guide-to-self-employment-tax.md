## What Is Self-Employment Tax?

When you work for yourself, you don’t just pay income tax — you also pay something called **self-employment tax**.

✅ Self-employment tax covers:

- **Social Security tax (12.4%)**  
- **Medicare tax (2.9%)**

Together, that’s **15.3%** of your net income — on top of regular income taxes.

## Why Do Freelancers and Business Owners Have to Pay It?

When you’re an employee, your employer pays half of these taxes for you.

But when you’re self-employed, you’re both the employer *and* the employee — so you pay the **full amount** yourself.

✅ That’s why it’s called “self-employment” tax — and why it feels so high.

## Who Has to Pay Self-Employment Tax?

If you earn **$400 or more** in net self-employment income during the year, you’re required to pay self-employment tax.

This includes:

- Freelancers  
- Contractors  
- Creators  
- Consultants  
- Anyone earning income through 1099s, tips, cash, or digital platforms

Even if you only freelance part-time, you’re still on the hook once you cross that $400 threshold.

## How to Calculate It

✅ Step-by-step:

1. **Start with your total income**  
   (e.g. $50,000 from freelance work)

2. **Subtract business expenses**  
   (e.g. $10,000 in write-offs)

3. **Net self-employment income = $40,000**

4. **Multiply by 92.35%**  
   (IRS adjustment to reflect the “employee share”)

5. **Multiply that by 15.3%**  
   (That’s your self-employment tax)

Example:

- $40,000 × 92.35% = $36,940  
- $36,940 × 15.3% = **$5,651** self-employment tax

✅ You may also owe federal and state income tax on top of that.

## When and How Do You Pay It?

You typically pay self-employment tax **through quarterly estimated payments** — not just once a year in April.

Quarterly deadlines:

- April 15  
- June 15  
- September 15  
- January 15 (of the following year)

If you wait until tax time to pay, you might owe **penalties and interest** for not paying as you go.

## Can You Deduct Any of It?

Yes!

✅ You can deduct **half** of your self-employment tax (7.65%) as an adjustment to income on your annual tax return.

This doesn’t reduce what you owe during the year, but it does lower your taxable income when you file.

## Why Self-Employment Tax Matters

Many freelancers and new business owners are caught off guard by how high their tax bill is.

✅ Understanding self-employment tax helps you:

- Avoid surprises  
- Set aside the right amount  
- Stay compliant  
- Make better pricing decisions

Knowing the rules gives you more control over your business and your money.
