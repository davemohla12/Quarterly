# Top Tax Deductions for Freelancers and Small Business Owners
<div style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 20px;">March 8, 2026</div>

**Want to save money on taxes? Here’s a simple guide to the most valuable deductions freelancers and small business owners can use to lower their tax bill.**

<img src="/images/blog/top-tax-deductions.png" alt="Top Tax Deductions" />

## Why Tax Deductions Matter

Tax deductions reduce your taxable income, meaning you pay taxes only on your **profit**, not your total earnings.

By knowing which expenses you can write off, you can:

- Lower your quarterly and year-end tax payments
- Keep more of what you earn
- Run a healthier business

## Common Freelancer and Small Business Deductions

✅ **Home Office Deduction**  
If you work from a dedicated space at home, you can deduct a portion of rent, utilities, and internet.

✅ **Business Equipment and Supplies**  
Laptops, phones, printers, software, and even office chairs can be deducted.

✅ **Health Insurance Premiums**  
If you pay for your own health insurance, you may be able to deduct premiums.

✅ **Travel and Meals**  
Business-related travel and meals (typically 50% of the cost) are deductible.

✅ **Marketing and Advertising**  
Website costs, social media ads, business cards — all count as marketing expenses.

✅ **Professional Services**  
Payments to accountants, lawyers, or consultants can be deducted.

✅ **Education and Training**  
Courses, books, and workshops related to your business are valid deductions.

## Keep Good Records

To claim these deductions, you need:

- Receipts or invoices
- Notes on the business purpose of the expense
- A simple system (like an app or spreadsheet) to track expenses

## What About Big Purchases?

For larger items (like a computer), you may be able to **deduct the full amount in the year of purchase** (Section 179) or **depreciate** it over several years.

## How Deductions Impact Quarterly Taxes

By factoring in deductions, you reduce your estimated quarterly tax payments.

Example:

- $80,000 gross income  
- $20,000 in deductions  
- $60,000 taxable income

You only pay tax on $60,000, not the full $80,000.

## Why It’s Worth Knowing

Every dollar you deduct is a dollar you keep.  

Smart tax planning can save you thousands each year and make running your business less stressful.
