## First, Don’t Panic

Missing a quarterly tax payment happens — especially if you’re new to freelancing or your income suddenly increased.

✅ The good news:  
You can catch up, minimize penalties, and get back on track without facing the wrath of the IRS.

The sooner you act, the better.

## What Happens When You Miss a Payment?

When you skip or underpay a quarterly tax deadline, the IRS may charge:

- ✅ **Late payment penalty** (0.5% of the unpaid amount per month)  
- ✅ **Interest** on the unpaid balance  
- ✅ **Underpayment penalty** at year-end if you didn’t pay enough overall

These fees add up the longer you wait — but they’re avoidable if you catch up quickly.

## Step 1: Estimate What You Should Have Paid

Recalculate what you owed for the quarter you missed.

✅ Use your income and expenses from that period  
✅ Apply your estimated tax rate (usually 25–30% of profit)  
✅ Include state taxes if applicable

If you're not sure, our app can back-calculate it for you.

## Step 2: Make the Payment ASAP

The IRS doesn’t require a special form — just pay what you owe directly online:

- ✅ [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal  
- ✅ Your state’s tax payment portal for state taxes

✅ Select the correct quarter and year when paying.  
The sooner you pay, the less you’ll owe in penalties and interest.

## Step 3: Adjust Your Next Payment

If you missed a quarter, make up the shortfall in your next quarterly payment.

✅ Example:

- Q2 payment missed: $3,000  
- Q3 estimate: $3,000  
→ Pay $6,000 in Q3 to stay on track

You can combine payments as long as you're still aiming to meet your total annual obligation.

## Step 4: Track What You Paid

Keep records of:

- Amounts paid  
- Dates  
- Confirmation numbers

✅ This helps when filing your annual return or if you need to file IRS Form 2210 to explain your payment history.

## Step 5: Consider Filing IRS Form 2210 (Optional)

If you:

- Made uneven income throughout the year, or  
- Had a valid reason for underpayment (e.g. natural disaster, illness)

…you may qualify for a **penalty reduction** or waiver using [IRS Form 2210](https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-2210).

## What If You Missed More Than One Payment?

Follow the same process — catch up ASAP, combine what you owe, and adjust your future payments.

✅ Don’t wait until April to deal with it. The longer you delay, the higher your penalties may be.
