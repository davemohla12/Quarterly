## Why Quarterly Deadlines Matter

If you’re self-employed, a freelancer, or earning side income without tax withheld, the IRS expects you to pay taxes **as you go** — not just at year-end.

✅ That means sending in **estimated payments** four times a year.

Missing these deadlines can lead to:

- Late payment penalties  
- Interest charges  
- A surprise tax bill in April

## The Quarterly Tax Deadlines

Each year, estimated taxes are due on the following dates:

- **April 15** — covers income earned Jan 1 – Mar 31  
- **June 15** — covers Apr 1 – May 31  
- **September 15** — covers Jun 1 – Aug 31  
- **January 15 (next year)** — covers Sep 1 – Dec 31

✅ If the deadline falls on a weekend or holiday, it’s pushed to the next business day.

These dates apply to **federal** taxes. Most states follow the same schedule, but check with your state tax agency to be sure.

## Who Needs to Pay?

You’re required to make quarterly tax payments if:

- You expect to owe at least **$1,000** in tax this year  
- You earn untaxed income (freelance, side hustle, contract work, etc.)  
- You don’t have enough tax withheld from a W-2 job

✅ This includes:

- Freelancers and consultants  
- Indie creators and course sellers  
- Side hustlers and gig workers  
- Small business owners and solopreneurs

## How Much Should You Pay?

A good rule of thumb is to set aside **25–30% of your net income** for taxes.

You can also calculate based on:

- 90% of what you expect to owe this year, or  
- 100% of last year’s total tax (110% if your income is over $150,000)

✅ These are the IRS “safe harbor” rules that help you avoid penalties.

## What Happens If You Miss a Deadline?

Missing a quarterly deadline can lead to:

- ✅ **Late payment penalty** (0.5% per month)  
- ✅ **Interest charges** on the unpaid amount  
- ✅ **Underpayment penalty** if you don’t meet safe harbor

Even if you pay in full by April, you can still owe penalties if you didn’t pay enough throughout the year.

## What If Your Income Changes?

You’re allowed to adjust your payments each quarter.

✅ If your income drops, lower your next payment  
✅ If your income increases, catch up in the next quarter  
✅ If you missed a payment, pay it as soon as possible

The IRS only cares that enough was paid by year-end — not that each payment was identical.

## How to Stay On Track

- ✅ Mark your calendar with the deadlines  
- ✅ Set aside tax money from each payment you receive  
- ✅ Use an app to calculate and track payments  
- ✅ Don’t wait until the last minute

Staying on top of quarterly deadlines prevents last-minute panic and expensive surprises.
