## Why the Start of the Year Matters

The first quarter sets the tone for the entire year — and that includes your taxes.

✅ Getting your quarterly tax plan in place now means:

- No scrambling at deadline time  
- No missed payments or penalties  
- Less stress and more clarity all year long

The earlier you get organized, the easier it is to stay on track.

## Step 1: Estimate Your Annual Income

Start by projecting how much you expect to earn this year from:

- Freelance or contract work  
- Consulting or coaching  
- Content creation  
- Product or subscription sales  
- Any other self-employed income

✅ Use last year as a guide, and factor in any growth, new clients, or planned changes.

## Step 2: Subtract Expected Business Expenses

Estimate your tax-deductible business expenses:

- Software and subscriptions  
- Marketing and advertising  
- Equipment or supplies  
- Travel and meals  
- Home office portion  
- Contractor payments

✅ Subtract these from your income to get your **expected net profit**.

## Step 3: Apply a Tax Rate

Use a combined rate to estimate what you’ll owe:

- 15.3% for self-employment tax  
- 10–24% for federal income tax (depending on your bracket)

✅ Most freelancers should set aside **25–30%** of their profit for taxes.

Example:

- $80,000 income – $20,000 expenses = $60,000 profit  
- 25% tax rate → $15,000 in estimated taxes for the year

## Step 4: Divide by Four

Take your annual estimate and divide it by four to get your quarterly payments.

✅ In the example above:  
$15,000 ÷ 4 = $3,750 per quarter

That’s your target for each estimated tax payment.

## Step 5: Mark Your Calendar

Quarterly tax deadlines:

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15** (of the following year)

✅ Add these to your calendar now so you’re never caught off guard.

## Step 6: Automate What You Can

- Set up a separate tax savings account  
- Move 25–30% of income into it as you get paid  
- Use a tracker or app to keep tabs on payments

✅ Consistency is easier than catching up.

## Step 7: Recheck Quarterly

Your income will likely fluctuate — and that’s okay.

✅ Recalculate before each payment to:

- Adjust for new income  
- Add new expenses  
- Stay accurate and avoid overpaying or underpaying
