## What Happens When You’re Late?

If you miss a quarterly tax deadline, the IRS may charge:

- ✅ **Late payment penalties** — about 0.5% of the unpaid amount per month  
- ✅ **Interest** — which compounds daily  
- ✅ **Underpayment penalties** — if you don’t meet safe harbor rules

The longer you wait, the more these penalties can grow.

But there’s good news — most of them are **avoidable or reducible** if you act quickly.

## Step 1: Pay As Soon As Possible

The IRS doesn’t require special paperwork to fix a missed deadline.

✅ Just make the payment as soon as you can:

- [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal taxes  
- Your state tax payment portal for state taxes

Even a **partial payment** helps reduce penalties and interest.

## Step 2: Catch Up in the Next Quarter

You can make up for a missed payment by increasing your next one.

✅ Example:  
- Missed Q2 payment: $2,000  
- Q3 estimate: $2,000  
→ Pay $4,000 in Q3

The IRS looks at your **total payments across the year**, not just individual quarters.

## Step 3: Check If You Qualify for Safe Harbor

The IRS offers penalty protection if you meet one of these:

- ✅ You paid **90% of your current year’s total tax**, or  
- ✅ You paid **100% of last year’s tax** (110% if you made over $150K)

If you qualify under either, you’re generally safe from underpayment penalties — even if your payments were uneven or late.

## Step 4: Recalculate Your Payments

If your income fluctuates, it’s smart to recalculate each quarter.

✅ This ensures you don’t:

- Overpay unnecessarily  
- Underpay and trigger penalties  
- Miss an opportunity to correct earlier mistakes

## Step 5: Consider Form 2210 (If You Need to Explain)

If you:

- Had unusual income timing (like a big Q4 spike), or  
- Had a valid reason for paying late (e.g. natural disaster, illness)

…you may want to file **IRS Form 2210** when you do your taxes. It lets you:

- Show that your underpayment was reasonable  
- Request a penalty reduction or waiver

You can do this yourself or ask a tax pro for help.

## Step 6: Avoid Future Penalties

The best way to avoid late penalties is to **stay ahead**:

- ✅ Mark deadlines on your calendar  
- ✅ Set aside tax money as you earn  
- ✅ Use reminders or an app  
- ✅ Recalculate if your income changes

Future you will thank you.
