## You’re Not Alone

Freelancers and solo business owners miss quarterly tax deadlines all the time — especially if you’re new to self-employment.

✅ The important thing is not to ignore it.

With the right steps, you can catch up, avoid bigger penalties, and stay on track for the rest of the year.

## Step 1: Don’t Wait — Pay ASAP

The longer you delay, the more penalties and interest you may owe.

✅ Make your payment as soon as possible using:

- [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal taxes  
- Your state’s online payment portal if you owe state estimated taxes

Even if you don’t have the full amount, paying something now can reduce what you owe later.

## Step 2: Calculate What You Owe

Use your income and expenses from the missed quarter to estimate your payment.

✅ Don’t guess — underpayment can lead to more penalties.

- Add up your net income for the quarter  
- Multiply by 25–30% for a rough tax estimate  
- Include state tax if applicable

If your numbers are unclear, our app can calculate this for you.

## Step 3: Watch for These Penalties

The IRS may charge:

- ✅ **Late payment penalty** (~0.5% of the unpaid amount per month)  
- ✅ **Interest** that compounds daily  
- ✅ **Underpayment penalty** at year-end if you didn’t pay enough throughout the year

These fees grow over time — another reason to catch up now.

## Step 4: Adjust Future Payments

You can make up for a missed quarter by increasing your next payment.

✅ Example:  
- Missed Q2 payment: $2,500  
- Q3 payment: $2,500  
→ Pay $5,000 in Q3 to catch up

The IRS cares about your **year-end total** — not whether each quarter was equal.

## Step 5: Document Your Payment

Keep records of:

- How much you paid  
- When you paid it  
- What quarter it was for  
- Confirmation numbers

✅ This helps when you file your tax return and protects you if the IRS ever asks.

## Step 6: Don’t Miss the Next One

Put future quarterly deadlines on your calendar now:

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15 (next year)**

✅ Set a reminder a week before each one.

## Step 7: Stay Consistent Going Forward

Use this moment as a reset.

- Recalculate your taxes each quarter  
- Set aside 25–30% of your income as you go  
- Use an app to automate the process and track your payments
