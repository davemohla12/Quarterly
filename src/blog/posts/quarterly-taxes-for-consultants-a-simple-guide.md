## Why Consultants Owe Quarterly Taxes

As a consultant, you’re likely getting paid by clients **without tax being withheld**.

✅ That means you’re responsible for paying your own:

- Federal income tax  
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)  
- Possibly state income tax

And the IRS doesn’t want you to wait until April — they want you to pay as you go.

## What Are Quarterly Taxes?

Quarterly taxes are **estimated tax payments** you send to the IRS (and sometimes your state) **four times a year**.

They cover your expected tax bill for the year — split into four installments:

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15** (of the next year)

Miss a deadline and you could face penalties — even if you pay in full later.

## How to Know If You Need to Pay

You’re required to pay quarterly taxes if:

- You expect to owe **at least $1,000** in taxes for the year  
- You earn income without tax withheld (like consulting fees)  
- You don’t have enough tax withheld from another job

✅ If consulting is your main income source — you almost certainly need to pay.

## Step-by-Step: How to Calculate What You Owe

### Step 1: Estimate Your Income

Add up all your expected consulting income for the year:

- Client retainers  
- Hourly project work  
- Strategy calls  
- Workshop fees  
- Affiliate commissions (if applicable)

✅ Use last year’s numbers as a baseline if you're unsure.

### Step 2: Subtract Business Expenses

Deduct any costs related to your consulting business, including:

- Software (Notion, Zoom, Slack, etc.)  
- Advertising or marketing spend  
- Subcontractor or VA payments  
- Travel and meals  
- Education or certifications  
- Home office portion

This gives you your **net income** (profit).

### Step 3: Apply a Tax Rate

Multiply your profit by an estimated tax rate — typically **25–30%** to cover both income and self-employment tax.

Example:

- $100,000 profit × 30% = $30,000 total estimated taxes

### Step 4: Divide by Four

Split that total into quarterly payments.

✅ In this example:  
$30,000 ÷ 4 = $7,500 per quarter

## What If Your Income Changes?

No problem — just update your estimate for the next quarter.

✅ Quarterly taxes are flexible:

- Made more? Increase your next payment  
- Made less? Reduce the next one  
- Missed a quarter? Catch up in the next one

## Where to Pay

- ✅ [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal taxes  
- ✅ Your state’s website for state tax payments  
- ✅ Keep records of each payment and confirmation

## Tips for Consultants

- Set aside 25–30% of every payment you receive  
- Use a separate savings account for taxes  
- Mark deadlines in your calendar  
- Use an app to stay on track and avoid guesswork
