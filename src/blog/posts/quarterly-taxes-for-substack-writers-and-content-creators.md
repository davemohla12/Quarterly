## Why Creators Owe Quarterly Taxes

If you earn money from Substack, YouTube, sponsorships, affiliate links, or digital products — and no one is withholding taxes — you’re responsible for paying them yourself.

✅ That includes:

- Federal income tax  
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)  
- Possibly state income tax

And the IRS expects you to pay these **four times a year**, not just in April.

## Who Needs to Pay Quarterly Taxes?

You must make estimated payments if:

- You expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in federal tax this year  
- You’re earning income without tax automatically withheld  
- You’re a solo creator, freelancer, or independent publisher

✅ Substack writers, podcasters, YouTubers, and indie creators often qualify — even if your business is still growing.

## Common Creator Income That Triggers Quarterly Taxes

- Substack subscriptions  
- Sponsorship deals  
- Affiliate commissions  
- YouTube AdSense  
- Patreon or Ko-fi donations  
- Product or course sales (Gumroad, Teachable, Podia)  
- Freelance writing, speaking, or consulting gigs  
- Tips from Buy Me a Coffee or Venmo

If you're receiving 1099s or direct payments with **no tax withheld**, you likely owe quarterly taxes.

## Step-by-Step: How to Calculate What You Owe

### Step 1: Estimate Your Annual Income

Add up your projected creator income across all platforms.

✅ Use last year’s data as a baseline — or average your last few months and project forward.

### Step 2: Subtract Business Expenses

Deduct anything you spend to run your creative business:

- Hosting and email software  
- Equipment and editing tools  
- Internet and subscriptions  
- Marketing, design, or contractor costs  
- Home office portion

This gives you your **net income** (profit).

### Step 3: Apply a Tax Rate

Multiply your profit by **25–30%** to estimate your total taxes, including:

- ~15.3% self-employment tax  
- 10–24% federal income tax, depending on your bracket

✅ Example:  
$40,000 net income × 25% = $10,000 in estimated taxes

### Step 4: Divide by 4

Split that amount into four equal payments across the year:

- $10,000 ÷ 4 = $2,500 per quarter

This is your base quarterly payment — unless your income changes significantly during the year.

## When Are Payments Due?

Quarterly tax deadlines:

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15** (of the following year)

✅ If you miss one, catch up as soon as you can to avoid penalties.

## What If Your Income Is Inconsistent?

Totally normal for creators.

✅ Just update your income estimate each quarter:  
- Earn more? Increase your payment  
- Earn less? Adjust downward

Quarterly taxes are flexible — as long as you meet your year-end total.

## Where to Pay

- [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal  
- Your state’s tax website for state payments  
- Save confirmations and payment records for tax time
