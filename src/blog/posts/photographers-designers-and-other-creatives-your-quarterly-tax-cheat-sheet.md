## Why Creatives Owe Quarterly Taxes

If you’re a freelance photographer, designer, illustrator, videographer, or other creative — and you get paid directly by clients — chances are, **no taxes are being withheld**.

✅ That means you’re responsible for:

- Federal income tax  
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)  
- State income tax (if applicable)

The IRS expects you to pay these **quarterly**, not just at year-end.

## Who Needs to Pay

You must make quarterly estimated tax payments if:

- You expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in federal tax this year  
- You earn income **without tax being withheld** (e.g. 1099s, Venmo, PayPal)  
- Your creative business is growing and generating consistent income

✅ This applies whether you’re full-time freelance or doing creative work on the side.

## What Counts as Taxable Creative Income?

- Client photography or design gigs  
- Licensing fees for your images or artwork  
- Retainers for branding or marketing projects  
- Creative direction or consulting  
- Event coverage (weddings, shoots, etc.)  
- Commissioned pieces or digital illustrations  
- Sales of creative assets (presets, templates, mockups)

If you’re receiving payments — and no taxes are being taken out — it counts.

## What Expenses Can You Deduct?

As a creative pro, you likely have lots of write-offs:

- Cameras, lighting, and lenses  
- Software (Adobe, Final Cut, Canva)  
- Website, domain, and hosting  
- Props, backdrops, and studio space  
- Travel for shoots or events  
- Courses, books, and industry events  
- Subcontractors or assistants  
- Home office portion

✅ Subtract these from your income to get your **net profit** — that’s what you’re taxed on.

## How to Estimate Your Quarterly Taxes

### Step 1: Project your annual income  
Look at how much you expect to earn this year from creative work.

### Step 2: Subtract deductible expenses  
Estimate your total business costs to get your profit.

### Step 3: Multiply by a tax rate  
✅ Use 25–30% to cover both income and self-employment taxes.

Example:  
$60,000 income – $15,000 expenses = $45,000 profit  
$45,000 × 25% = **$11,250 total estimated tax**

### Step 4: Divide by 4  
$11,250 ÷ 4 = $2,812.50 per quarter

That’s what you’ll pay each quarter — unless your income changes, in which case, adjust accordingly.

## Quarterly Tax Deadlines

- **April 15**  
- **June 15**  
- **September 15**  
- **January 15** (of the next year)

✅ Put these on your calendar to avoid late fees.

## How to Pay

- [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay) for federal taxes  
- Your state’s website for state payments  
- Keep receipts and confirmation emails

## Tips for Staying on Track

- Open a separate account to save for taxes  
- Set aside 25–30% of every payment you receive  
- Use a tracker, spreadsheet, or app to stay organized  
- Recalculate each quarter if your income fluctuates
