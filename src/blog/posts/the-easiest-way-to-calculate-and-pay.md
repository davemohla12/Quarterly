## Why Quarterly Taxes Feel Overwhelming

Most freelancers and small business owners worry about:

- How much to pay  
- When to pay  
- Where to pay  
- What happens if they get it wrong

Good news: you don’t need to hire an accountant or tax pro to handle quarterly taxes — if you follow a clear, simple process.

## Step 1: Estimate Your Annual Income

✅ Add up all sources of income you expect for the year:  
Freelance projects, consulting, product sales, online courses, affiliate income, side hustles.

## Step 2: Subtract Expenses

✅ Identify deductible business expenses:
- Equipment and software
- Home office costs
- Marketing and advertising
- Travel and meals
- Professional services

This gives you your **net profit** — what you actually owe tax on.

## Step 3: Calculate Estimated Taxes

✅ Apply tax rates:
- ~15.3% self-employment tax
- Federal income tax (10–24% for most freelancers)
- State income tax (if applicable)

A good rule of thumb is to set aside **25–30%** of your net profit.

## Step 4: Divide by Four

✅ Divide your total estimated taxes by four to get your quarterly payment.

Example:

$10,000 estimated tax ÷ 4 = $2,500 per quarter

## Step 5: Pay the IRS

✅ Pay online using:
- IRS Direct Pay (for individuals)
- EFTPS (for businesses)

Make sure to select the right tax year and quarter when submitting payment.

## Step 6: Stay Organized

✅ Keep a record of:
- Payment confirmation numbers
- Dates paid
- Amounts paid

This will help at year-end when you file your tax return.
