## Why Comparing Options Matters

If you're self-employed, you have three main ways to handle quarterly taxes:

- Use a dedicated app (like Zenguider)  
- Use tax software (like TurboTax)  
- Do it yourself with spreadsheets and IRS forms

Each has pros and cons depending on your experience, income, and how hands-on you want to be.

## Option 1: Zenguider

✅ Built specifically for quarterly tax payers  
✅ Designed for freelancers, solo entrepreneurs, and creators  
✅ Handles both federal and state estimates  
✅ Automatically adjusts for income changes  
✅ Reminds you before deadlines  
✅ One-time annual price (not a subscription)

**Best for:**  
People who want a fast, no-brainer tool that simplifies the process and minimizes the risk of penalties.

**Pros:**  
- Extremely easy to use  
- No tax jargon  
- Works year-round — not just at tax time  
- Built-in logic for safe harbor and penalty avoidance

**Cons:**  
- Doesn’t file your annual return (yet — it’s for quarterly taxes only)

## Option 2: TurboTax

✅ Well-known tax filing software  
✅ Lets you enter income and expenses  
✅ Generates Form 1040-ES vouchers

**Best for:**  
People already using TurboTax for year-end taxes who don’t mind manually handling their quarterly payments.

**Pros:**  
- Familiar brand  
- Integrated with year-end tax filing  
- Good for simple tax situations

**Cons:**  
- Not built for quarterly taxes  
- No real-time tracking  
- Doesn’t auto-adjust when income changes  
- No reminders or deadline alerts  
- Can be expensive (especially for self-employed versions)

## Option 3: Doing It Yourself

✅ Download Form 1040-ES  
✅ Use spreadsheets or manual calculations  
✅ Pay directly at IRS.gov

**Best for:**  
Experienced filers who love spreadsheets and want full control.

**Pros:**  
- Free (except your time)  
- Total flexibility  
- Teaches you how the system works

**Cons:**  
- Easy to make mistakes  
- No reminders or automation  
- No help if your income changes  
- You’re on your own with penalties
