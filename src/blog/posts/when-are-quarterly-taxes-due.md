## What Are Quarterly Taxes?

If you’re self-employed, the IRS requires you to pay estimated taxes throughout the year — not just at tax time.

These are called **quarterly estimated tax payments** and they cover:

- Federal income tax
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)
- Sometimes state income tax

## Who Needs to Pay?

If you’re a freelancer, creator, solo entrepreneur, or small business owner and expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in taxes for the year, you’re likely required to pay quarterly.

## 2025 IRS Quarterly Tax Deadlines

✅ **1st Quarter (January–March income)**  
Due: **April 15, 2025**

✅ **2nd Quarter (April–May income)**  
Due: **June 16, 2025** (June 15 is a Sunday)

✅ **3rd Quarter (June–August income)**  
Due: **September 15, 2025**

✅ **4th Quarter (September–December income)**  
Due: **January 15, 2026**

## 2026 IRS Quarterly Tax Deadlines

✅ **1st Quarter (January–March income)**  
Due: **April 15, 2026**

✅ **2nd Quarter (April–May income)**  
Due: **June 15, 2026**

✅ **3rd Quarter (June–August income)**  
Due: **September 15, 2026**

✅ **4th Quarter (September–December income)**  
Due: **January 15, 2027**

## What Happens If You Miss a Deadline?

The IRS charges **penalties and interest** if you pay late or underpay.

Even if you pay your full tax bill in April, you can still owe penalties for missed or late quarterly payments.

## How to Stay On Track

- Mark deadlines on your calendar
- Set aside ~25–30% of income as you earn it
- Use a calculator or app to estimate accurate payments
- Pay early if you’re unsure — it reduces penalties

## Do States Have Separate Deadlines?

Most states follow the federal schedule, but some have their own deadlines.  
Check your state’s tax website or use a tool that includes state tax calculations.

## Why Deadlines Matter

Missing quarterly tax deadlines can lead to:

- Extra costs
- IRS notices and penalties
- Stress at tax time

Staying on top of deadlines helps keep your business running smoothly.
