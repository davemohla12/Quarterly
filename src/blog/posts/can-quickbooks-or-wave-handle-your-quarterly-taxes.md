## What QuickBooks and Wave Are Great At

✅ **Bookkeeping and invoicing:**  
Both QuickBooks and Wave do a solid job tracking income and expenses.

✅ **Profit and loss reports:**  
You can easily view your earnings and business costs, which helps during tax time.

✅ **Bank syncing and categorization:**  
They make it easier to keep tabs on your financials throughout the year.

## Where They Fall Short on Quarterly Taxes

Despite their strengths, these platforms weren’t built with **freelancer tax compliance** as a core feature — especially when it comes to quarterly taxes.

Here’s what they **don’t do well**:

🚫 **No automatic quarterly tax reminders**  
🚫 **No real-time estimated tax calculations**  
🚫 **No safe harbor checks to avoid IRS penalties**  
🚫 **No direct payment links to the IRS or states**  
🚫 **No multi-state income handling or sourcing rules**

## What You’ll Still Need to Do Manually

If you use QuickBooks or Wave, you’ll likely have to:

- Estimate taxes on your own  
- Track deadlines separately  
- Log into IRS Direct Pay each quarter  
- Hope your estimates are close enough to avoid penalties

That’s a lot of room for error — especially if your income changes quarter to quarter.

## Real User Feedback

Many freelancers start with tools like QuickBooks or Wave, only to realize:

> “It tracked my expenses, but I still had no idea what to pay in quarterly taxes.”  
> “I missed a deadline because it never told me when payments were due.”  
> “I still needed another app or a CPA to help calculate what I owed.”

## So, Can They Handle Quarterly Taxes?

Technically? You can **extract the info you need** and do the rest yourself.

But they don’t walk you through it. They weren’t designed for the solo freelancer trying to stay compliant **without an accountant**.

It’s like using a spreadsheet to track workouts — helpful, but you still need to know the right moves.
