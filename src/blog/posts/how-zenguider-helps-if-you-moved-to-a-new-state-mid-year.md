## Why Moving Mid-Year Gets Tricky

If you moved to a new state this year — or split time between states — your quarterly tax situation gets more complex.

Each state may expect a portion of your:

- **Income tax**
- **Self-employment tax**
- **Quarterly estimated payments**

You might owe taxes in **both states**, depending on when and where you earned income.

## Most Tools Don’t Handle This Well

Most quarterly tax tools assume you stayed in one place all year. That’s a problem if:

- You moved from a **no-income-tax state** to a high-tax state  
- You worked remotely in multiple places  
- You changed your legal residency partway through the year

Without proper state allocation, your tax estimates could be way off.
