# Do You Need to Pay State Quarterly Taxes? A State-by-State Breakdown
<div style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 20px;">March 12, 2026</div>

**Many freelancers and solo entrepreneurs know about federal quarterly taxes — but what about state taxes? Here’s what you need to know, state by state.**

<img src="/images/blog/do-you-need-to-pay-quarterly-taxes.png" alt="Do You Need to Pay State Quarterly Taxes" />

## Why State Taxes Matter

Federal taxes aren’t the only taxes you owe as a self-employed professional.  
Depending on where you live and work, your **state** may also require:

- Income tax
- Self-employment tax
- Estimated quarterly payments

Missing these can lead to state-level penalties and interest, just like with the IRS.

## Do All States Have Income Tax?

No. As of now, these states **do not** impose personal income tax:

- Alaska
- Florida
- Nevada
- South Dakota
- Texas
- Washington
- Wyoming

If you live in these states, you generally don’t need to make state quarterly payments.

## What About States With Income Tax?

If you live in a state with income tax and expect to owe taxes over a certain threshold, you likely need to make **state estimated tax payments** too.

Examples:

- **California:** Pay if you owe $500+ in tax.
- **New York:** Pay if you owe $300+.
- **Illinois:** Pay if you owe $500+.

Each state sets its own rules, thresholds, forms, and deadlines.

## When Are State Quarterly Payments Due?

Most states follow the **same schedule as the IRS**:

- April 15
- June 15
- September 15
- January 15

But a few states have different dates or requirements — always check your state’s tax website.

## How to Calculate State Quarterly Taxes

- Start with your **net income** (after expenses)
- Apply your **state income tax rate**
- Divide by four to get the quarterly amount

Many online calculators and tax software can help estimate this accurately.

## What If You Work in Multiple States?

If you earn income across multiple states, you may owe taxes in each — based on where the income was sourced.

This can get complex, so it’s worth using an app or consulting a tax pro to get it right.

## Why It’s Worth Staying On Top of

State tax penalties can add up just like IRS penalties.  
By making estimated payments on time, you avoid:

- Late fees
- Interest charges
- Year-end cash flow surprises
