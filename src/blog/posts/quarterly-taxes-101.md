## What Are Quarterly Taxes?

When you work for yourself, taxes aren’t automatically withheld from your income. Instead, the IRS expects you to estimate and pay taxes yourself — four times a year. These are called **quarterly estimated tax payments**.

In short, you’re paying:

- Federal income tax
- Self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)
- Sometimes state income tax

## Who Needs to Pay Quarterly Taxes?

If you’re a:

- Freelancer
- Consultant
- Solo entrepreneur
- Creator
- Side hustler

… and you expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in taxes for the year, the IRS likely expects quarterly payments.

## When Are Quarterly Taxes Due?

Mark your calendar for these four key dates each year:

- **April 15** — for income earned Jan–March
- **June 15** — for income earned April–May
- **September 15** — for income earned June–August
- **January 15 (next year)** — for income earned Sept–Dec

Missing these deadlines can result in penalties.

## How Do You Calculate Quarterly Taxes?

✅ Estimate your total income for the year  
✅ Subtract deductions + expenses  
✅ Calculate tax on the estimated profit  
✅ Divide by four

Sound complicated? That’s why many freelancers use a **quarterly tax calculator** or app to make this easy.

## What Happens If You Don’t Pay?

The IRS charges **penalties and interest** if you underpay or miss deadlines. Even if you pay everything at year-end, you can still owe penalties for missed quarterly payments.

## Tips to Make Quarterly Taxes Simple

- **Set aside ~25–30% of income** as you earn it
- **Use software or apps** to estimate payments accurately
- **Mark deadlines on your calendar**
- **Don’t overpay “just to be safe”** — stay precise

## Why It’s Worth Paying Attention

Quarterly taxes aren’t just a rule — they protect you from surprise bills, IRS penalties, and cash flow headaches. Handling them confidently is part of running a smooth, professional business.
