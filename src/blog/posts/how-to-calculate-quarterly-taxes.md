# How to Calculate Quarterly Taxes If You Earn from Multiple States or Sources
<div style="margin-top: 5px; margin-bottom: 20px;">March 5, 2026</div>

**Do you earn income from clients in different states or have multiple income streams? Here’s a simple guide to calculating your quarterly taxes accurately — without the headache.**

<img src="/images/blog/how-to-calculate-quarterly-taxes.webp" alt="How to Calculate Quarterly Taxes If You Earn from Multiple States or Sources" />

## Why Multi-State or Multi-Source Income Matters

When you’re a freelancer or small business owner, your income might come from:

- Clients in different states
- A mix of freelance work, consulting, products, or digital services
- Side hustles or passive income streams

This makes tax calculation a bit more complex — but with the right steps, it’s manageable.

## Step 1: Add Up All Your Income

✅ Combine all income sources:
- Freelance projects
- Consulting gigs
- Product sales
- Online courses
- Affiliate income

You need your **total gross income** across all streams.

## Step 2: Subtract Business Expenses

✅ Combine business expenses across all sources:
- Equipment and tools
- Marketing costs
- Professional services
- Travel, meals, home office

This gives you your **net taxable profit**.

## Step 3: Allocate Income Across States

✅ If you work across multiple states:
- Determine how much income was earned in each state.
- This can be based on where the client is, where the work was performed, or state-specific rules.

Some states require you to file as a **non-resident** if you earned income there.

## Step 4: Apply Federal and State Tax Rates

✅ Calculate:
- Federal income tax + self-employment tax (~15.3%)
- State income taxes (if applicable)

Many tax tools or calculators can help automate this part, especially for multi-state situations.

## Step 5: Divide by Four

✅ Divide the total estimated tax by four to get your quarterly payment amount.

If state taxes apply, you may need to make **separate state payments** alongside your federal payment.

## What About Withholding?

If you have W-2 income or retirement withdrawals with tax withholding, factor that in — you may owe less in estimated taxes.

## Tips for Multi-State Filers

- Keep detailed records of where income comes from.
- Check each state’s tax rules — they vary!
- Use a tool or professional help if you’re unsure.

## Why Getting It Right Matters

Multi-source and multi-state income increases your tax complexity — but it’s solvable.

Accurate estimates help you:

- Avoid IRS and state penalties
- Avoid overpaying or underpaying
- Keep more of your hard-earned money
