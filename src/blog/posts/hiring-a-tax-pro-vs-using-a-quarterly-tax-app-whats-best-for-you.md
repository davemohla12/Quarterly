## Why This Decision Matters

If you’re self-employed or running a solo business, you’ve probably wondered:

> Should I hire an accountant or just use an app to handle my quarterly taxes?

The truth? Both can work — but the right choice depends on your needs, your budget, and how complex your finances are.

## Option 1: Hiring a Tax Pro

✅ Great if you have complicated income  
✅ Helpful for multi-state issues, multiple businesses, or major life changes  
✅ They can file your annual return too

**Pros:**  
- Personalized advice  
- Can handle messy or changing situations  
- Peace of mind if you’re not confident doing it yourself

**Cons:**  
- Expensive — often $300–$1,000 just for quarterly estimates  
- Requires back-and-forth meetings or emails  
- You’re still on the hook to pay on time

**Best for:**  
High-income earners, people with complex business setups, or anyone who prefers hands-off help.

## Option 2: Using a Quarterly Tax App

✅ Fast, affordable, and built for simplicity  
✅ Calculates what you owe based on your income  
✅ Sends reminders before deadlines  
✅ Designed for freelancers, consultants, and solo business owners

**Pros:**  
- Super easy to use  
- Much cheaper than a tax pro  
- Built-in logic to help avoid penalties  
- Great for first-timers and busy entrepreneurs

**Cons:**  
- Doesn’t file your full annual return  
- May not cover highly complex situations (e.g. foreign income, partnerships)

**Best for:**  
Solo workers with straightforward income who want a simple way to stay compliant and penalty-free.
